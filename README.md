# Retail Sales Dashboard (Excel)

## Project Overview
This project analyses retail sales data using Microsoft Excel and presents the results in an interactive dashboard. The aim was to identify key trends in sales performance, profitability, and product-level contributions across different regions and categories.

## Dataset
The project uses the Sample Superstore dataset, which contains transactional sales data including order dates, regions, categories, sub-categories, sales, profit, and quantity.

## Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel
- Pivot Tables
- Pivot Charts
- Slicers
- Excel formulas (YEAR, TEXT, SUM, COUNTA)

## Project Process
- Cleaned and structured raw transactional data in Excel
- Created calculated columns including Year, Month, and Profit Margin
- Built pivot tables to analyse:
  - Sales by region
  - Monthly sales trends
  - Profit by category
  - Sales by sub-category
- Developed visualisations using charts to present key metrics
- Implemented slicers (Region, Category, Year) to enable dynamic filtering
- Designed a clean, interactive dashboard to display insights

## Key Insights

- The West region generated the highest overall sales (£739k), followed by the East (£691k), indicating stronger performance in these regions compared to Central and South.

- Technology was the most profitable category (£146k), significantly outperforming Office Supplies and Furniture, highlighting stronger margins within technology-based products.

- When filtering to the West region and Technology category, high-value sub-categories such as Phones, Accessories, and Machines dominate revenue, indicating a concentration of sales within a small number of products.

- In contrast, filtering to the Central region and Office Supplies category shows lower overall revenue contribution, with sales spread more evenly across sub-categories such as Binders, Paper, and Storage, suggesting less concentration and lower individual product impact.

- Sales exhibit clear seasonal patterns, with peaks in March, September, November, and December, indicating periods of increased demand throughout the year.

- Despite contributing to total sales, categories such as Furniture generate significantly lower profit (£19k), suggesting higher costs or lower margins compared to Technology and Office Supplies.

- Overall, the data suggests that focusing on high-margin categories like Technology in high-performing regions such as the West could significantly improve overall profitability.
